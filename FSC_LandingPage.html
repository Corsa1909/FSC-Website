Understanding Your Legal Protection

What Is the Amparo — and Why Does It Matter?

This is the most important thing to understand before starting any surrogacy program in Mexico.

Will S. — Single Father, 2023Holding his son and the Amparo birth certificate — issued in his name only, two weeks after birth.

Mexico doesn't have a federal surrogacy law. That's not a secret — but it's not always the first thing agencies tell you. What it means in practice: the legal protection for your parental rights depends entirely on how your program handles the Amparo, and whether the team behind it has done this many times before or just a few.

The Amparo is a federal court order, granted before birth, that instructs the civil registry to issue the birth certificate in your name(s) only. The surrogate's name does not appear. There is no legal ambiguity when you return home.

The birth certificate Will is holding in that photo took under two weeks to arrive after his son was born. His name only. No surrogate listed. That document is what made everything possible for him — re-entry, citizenship, parentage. That's what the Amparo does.
